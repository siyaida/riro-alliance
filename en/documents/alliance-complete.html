1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

1.1 Overview

RIRO is a strategic business alliance between Ridha (Bel Décor) — a glass decoration expert with 15+ years of production mastery — and Anthony (MEZZO GRP) — a commercial strategist with premium design capabilities, showroom infrastructure, and investment capital.

Structured as a SARL (Société à Responsabilité Limitée) under Tunisian law, RIRO targets the underserved glass decoration and personalization market across four high-potential segments: personalized glass objects, grande distribution packaging, cosmetics bottles, and olive oil bottles.

1.2 The Opportunity

Tunisia's glass decoration market lacks a structured, quality-driven industrial player. Growing demand across personalization, cosmetics, and olive oil export — combined with no dominant competitor — creates a significant first-mover opportunity. RIRO fills this gap by combining craft expertise with commercial infrastructure.

2. STRATEGIC CONTEXT

2.1 Tunisia Economic Overview

Tunisia has a diversified economy with key strengths in manufacturing, agriculture (particularly olive oil), and services. The country offers:

  • Strategic location: Gateway between Europe and Africa
  • Skilled labor force: Competitive costs relative to European markets
  • Free trade agreements: EU Association Agreement, various bilateral treaties
  • Growing domestic consumption: Rising middle class with increasing demand for premium products
  • Export infrastructure: Established ports and logistics for Mediterranean trade

2.2 Glass & Packaging Industry

The Tunisian glass industry serves both domestic and export markets. Key characteristics:

  • Import dependency: Many premium decorated glass products are imported, creating substitution opportunity
  • Local production gap: Limited local capacity for high-quality glass decoration and personalization
  • Growing segments: Cosmetics packaging, olive oil branding, and personalized products growing faster than GDP
  • Competitive advantage: Lower labor costs enable competitive pricing versus European decorated glass

2.3 Market Drivers

  1. Olive oil sector: Tunisia exports 100,000-300,000 tons annually. Premium branded bottles add 30-50% to product value.
  2. Cosmetics growth: North African beauty market growing 6-8% annually. Local brands increasingly demand local packaging.
  3. Personalization trend: Wedding culture, corporate gifting, and events create steady demand for custom glass.
  4. Grande distribution expansion: Retail chains modernizing and demanding standardized packaging solutions.

5.2 Asset Structure (Leased Model)

A critical design choice: assets are leased to the company, not contributed as capital.

┌─────────────────────────────────┐
│          RIRO SARL              │
│     (Operating Company)         │
│                                 │
│  Leases machines ← Ridha       │
│  Leases premises ← Anthony     │
│  Cash investment ← Anthony     │
│                                 │
│  Revenue → Shared per agreement │
└─────────────────────────────────┘

Why this structure:

  • Asset protection: Machines and premises remain personal property
  • Downside protection: If the company fails, each partner retains their assets
  • Flexibility: Lease terms can be adjusted without changing company structure
  • Tax efficiency: Lease payments are deductible expenses for the company
  • Compte courant: Initial lease payments build shareholder loan balances, providing additional recovery mechanisms

5.3 Governance Overview

  • Co-gérance: Both Partners serve as co-managers
  • Domain autonomy: Each director operates independently within their domain
  • Major decisions: Require unanimous consent (>5,000 TND expenses, hires, contracts >10,000 TND)
  • Steering committee: Monthly meetings for strategic oversight
  • Deadlock resolution: Mediation → Arbitration (Tunisian Chamber of Commerce)
