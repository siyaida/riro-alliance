2. PURPOSE / OBJET

The Partners hereby agree to establish a joint business venture under the name "DecoLux", structured as a Société à Responsabilité Limitée (SARL) under Tunisian law, for the purpose of:

  • Glass decoration and personalization services
  • Production and sale of decorated glass products (personalized objects, cosmetics bottles, olive oil bottles, grande distribution packaging)
  • Commercial development of glass decoration solutions in Tunisia and beyond

This Memorandum of Understanding sets forth the principal terms upon which the Partners intend to form, operate, and govern the DecoLux alliance.

5.3 Asset Lease Structure / Structure de Location

Critical provision: Machines and premises are NOT contributed to the company as capital (apports en nature). They are leased to DecoLux SARL via separate lease agreements. This ensures:

  • Assets remain the personal property of their respective owners
  • In case of dissolution, assets return to their original owners
  • Company cannot pledge, encumber, or dispose of leased assets
  • Lease payments contribute to each Partner's compte courant associé

11. NON-COMPETE & NON-SOLICITATION / NON-CONCURRENCE ET NON-SOLLICITATION

11.1 During the Alliance

Neither Partner shall, directly or indirectly:

  • Engage in glass decoration or personalization activities outside DecoLux
  • Operate or invest in a competing business in Tunisia
  • Divert DecoLux clients or opportunities to personal ventures

11.2 Post-Termination

  • Duration: 2 years following effective termination date
  • Geographic scope: Republic of Tunisia
  • Sector scope: Glass decoration, personalization, and packaging
  • Employee non-solicitation: Neither Partner shall recruit or solicit DecoLux employees for 2 years post-termination
  • Client non-solicitation: Neither Partner shall actively solicit DecoLux clients for 18 months post-termination

11.3 Exceptions

  • Pre-existing client relationships maintained outside DecoLux's scope
  • Activities expressly approved in writing by the other Partner
  • Passive investments (<5%) in publicly traded companies

14. EXIT STRATEGY / STRATÉGIE DE SORTIE

14.1 Voluntary Exit / Sortie Volontaire

  • Notice period: 6 months written notice
  • Transition period: Additional 3 months for operational handover
  • Buyout right: Remaining Partner has first right of refusal to purchase departing Partner's shares

14.2 Valuation Method / Méthode d'Évaluation

Share value determined as the average of:

  1. Book value: Net asset value per most recent audited financial statements
  2. Earnings multiple: 3× average annual net profit of the preceding 2 years

Independent valuation by a mutually agreed expert if Partners cannot agree on price.

14.3 Forced Exit Triggers / Déclencheurs de Sortie Forcée

Either Partner may trigger forced buyout upon:

  • Material breach: Not cured within 30 calendar days of written notice
  • Insolvency: Filing for bankruptcy or inability to pay debts
  • Criminal conviction: Conviction for fraud, embezzlement, or other crime affecting the business
  • Prolonged non-performance: Failure to fulfill role obligations for 3+ consecutive months without valid reason
  • Breach of non-compete: Engaging in competing activities

14.4 Asset Distribution / Répartition des Actifs

Upon dissolution or exit:

  1. Leased assets: Return to their respective owners (machines to Ridha, premises to Anthony)
  2. Company debts: Settled from company assets first
  3. Compte courant: Reimbursed to each Partner per their balance
  4. Remaining company assets: Distributed proportionally to shareholding
  5. DecoLux brand: Usage rights negotiated or brand retired

14.5 Client Transition / Transition Clients

  • 90-day transition period for client communications
  • Joint communication to clients about transition
  • Client choice respected — neither Partner may coerce client decisions
  • All client data remains accessible to both Partners during transition

15. DISPUTE RESOLUTION / RÉSOLUTION DES LITIGES

Step 1: Good Faith Negotiation / Négociation de Bonne Foi

  • Duration: 30 calendar days
  • Partners meet in person to resolve the dispute
  • Both Partners must participate in good faith

Step 2: Mediation / Médiation

  • Institution: Chambre de Commerce et d'Industrie de Tunis
  • Duration: Maximum 60 calendar days
  • Cost: Shared equally between Partners
  • Language: French

Step 3: Arbitration / Arbitrage

  • Institution: Centre d'Arbitrage et de Conciliation Industriels (CACI), Tunisia
  • Rules: CACI arbitration rules
  • Arbitrator(s): Single arbitrator mutually selected, or panel of 3 if parties cannot agree
  • Language: French (with Arabic as official legal language per Tunisian law)
  • Location: Tunis, Tunisia
  • Decision: Final and binding
  • Cost: Borne by the losing party, unless arbitrator decides otherwise
